JQuery Mobile App Development

I have been using JQuery Mobile for about a year now so I thought I would write up my thoughts on it. I also will include some useful add-ins I have used in conjunction to JQuery Mobile in my development travels.

Reasons to go there

Prototypes: Great for quick prototypes.

Because it is so quick to get started with a JQuery Mobile project I will often use it to create quick prototypes around a concept I am working on. While the final product may end out being created with a different technology if you need to quickly get something running to help visualize an idea then JQuery Mobile will defiantly be your friend.

Ease of learning: Easy to get started and understand.

As long as you know a bit of simple html and can follow the setup instructions given you can get rolling fairly quickly with this simple framework. The docs are relatively easy to follow and is a good deal of ideas flowing in place like stackoverflow. Oh and there is the provided theme roller to really help get things moving.

Reasons not to go there

Your a control freak: ok maybe a little strong :)

Using the JQuery Mobile framework things can begin to all look a bit the same, and if you what fine control over the look of your app you would be better served looking else where rather than fight with it.

You want full interaction with the phone:

This obviously goes for all web apps but there comes a point where if you want to do certain things with the users phone you need to either move to a native app for the target phones or use a technology such as PhoneGap that will convert your web app to a native app.

Projects I found useful

Icons

Fontawsome

Great looking simple icons that you can integrate with your web app.

Image Gallery

Photoswipe

A simple great looking web app enabled photo gallery

Maps

jquery-ui-map

A full api that allows you to easily integrate mapping features into your web app.

Making it native

PhoneGap

If you reach the point you need to go native (phone apps of course) then PhoneGap can be a great way to go.

3 Important Choices When Creating Your App

If you are considering building aor having an app created for you here are a couple of pointers of what you should think about before getting stuck in.

I have been dealing with a client recently that started their app project with great gusto and a ‘it needs to be built yesterday’ attitude that is very common. I was to build the UI for them using thier existing app as a base - now that we have gone passed thier imposed deadline of having the app ‘done’. I gave them a call to see how it was all going - well it seemed to be comming un-stuck at the seams as they batteled to understand what they are building.

So what questions should they have thought while they built thier app?

Who is your app for?

An obvious question but the answer can often be tainted by what you believe people want or should want from you app.

In the instance above the app was to be built with a friendly user interface to access environmental monitoring data. True to form the first customer just wanted access to the raw data, but this is not to say in the future other clients will want that fancy user interface.

What platforms will you target?

Obvioulsy this will be somewhat answered when you know who your creating your app for. These days you will often see apps created for both the iPhone and Andriod - but who are your users. In one instance it turned out that while many of the clients aspired to own an iPhone the phones thier work provided were in-fact Blackberries.

Will it continue?

Is this a one-off app that will be put out but you don’t intend to upgrade or extends over time?

My parents, probably along with many in their generation, have recently entered the world of smart phones and apps with gusto. Its cool to watch them get excited over all of these apps and install them, get frustrated, keep the gems they find and uninstall the others (OK so I try and make sure they get rid of the ones they don’t use :)).

So what is the big deal then? well to me the big deal is the convenience - your phone is never to far from you and therefore neither are apps that you have installed on it. Being able to track your run or ride and look at it afterward, or do a bit of reading while you are delayed or check details of an event with out having to carry a bunch of paper with you. Now that is a big deal in my books - convenience on your terms.

Now what about all those gimmicky apps you install and then feel dirty afterward? Well in the coming years as the pure novelty of apps wears off you will see a much higher quality and consistency of app in regards to both use and content. Because more and more the same will happen with apps as did with the web - remember when there was a glut of those websites with flashing titles and buttons all over the show?

What Types of Phone Apps Are There

When you think about having an app developed there are options about how the app can be developed that can be worth considering before you jump in.

Having created apps both ways listed below there are definitely benefits to each. For me personally my preference is web apps because I can more easily control the development and updating of the application - but in saying that there are many applications that are not suited to the web application approach.

Native Apps

Native apps are were you create the app using the devices native development language which differ on the iPhone, Android and other devices.

  • Use full complement of native functions such as camera, accelerometer, address book …
  • You can use storage on the phone for any photos/files.
  • The app will run faster.
  • App store approval process can hinder - while it helps monitor the quality it also can causes big headaches when rapidly developing new functionality.

Web Apps

With web technologies getting far richer there aren’t many things that you can’t do these days.

  • Create the app once for all platforms - you are targeting the phones browser so you can get greater coverage with one version of your app.
  • Quicker and easier to update - you can very quickly publish changes and fixes to your app as they aren’t held up by app stores.

Other options

There are always other options :) With phone apps you can create a web app and then using certain tools convert it to a native app. This can help with the issue of creating apps that work on multiple devices - solving this issue can be a blessing depending on the audience of your app and allocated budget.
